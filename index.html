What...

It is a pseudo-journalistic product with the purpose of deceiving or misinforming. Fake news is mainly composed of altered facts presented as truths or certainties.

Who...

Fake news can be created by any intentioned person, willing to harm or obtain some benefit by misinforming; individuals, politicians, social movements or even average people can be the creators of such false content. Fake news may affect any contemporary internaut, especially frequent social media users, these users get misinformed or bothered by low tier content. It has been suggested by researchers that fake news can reach over 1500 people six times faster than any real news would. Another research done by the academics of MIT established that fake news was 70% more likely to be retweeted.

When...

Fake news can emerge at any given time due to constant news circulation, emergence is entirely dependent on the intentions of the misinformers and the misinformers

Where...

Fake news’ occurrence does not have a territorial boundary, this problem is found all around the globe due to globalization and the availability of networking services.

Why...

Misinforming news are created to cause harm or to benefit its creator who has motivations to act upon. Generally being propaganda, corporative grieving or provocation. Nonetheless, the aforementioned reasons that justify fake news are not necessarily unique. Fake news might also persist due to rushed press and newspapers that do not document or register properly and event and end up publishing something incomplete, false or inconsistent.

How...

Fake news is mostly created and distributed throughout contemporary social media, it can also be found on Tv, radio or any other communication medium.
